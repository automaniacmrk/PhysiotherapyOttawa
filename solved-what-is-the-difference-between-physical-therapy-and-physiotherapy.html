Solved: What is the Difference between Physical Therapy and Physiotherapy?

Physical therapy and physiotherapy can be used interchangeably for the majority of people. This means that most people don't know the difference between the two. Many countries use physiotherapy to refer to the practice. However, in the United States, physical therapy is the dominant term and physiotherapy can either describe physical therapy or be used to describe a therapy with little difference to physical therapy. There is very little difference in physical therapy and physiotherapy.

Even so, a small, but notable difference between physical therapy and physiotherapy could be claimed. Some health care workers believe that physiotherapy can be described as a hands-on method of rehabilitation therapy. The physiotherapist employs manual techniques to assist the patient. It could also include deepening your stretches or other methods. Others would argue that physical therapy can be viewed as primarily an exercise-based treatment. There are numerous benefits to physical therapy, regardless of any disagreements.
